ModalNeRF: Neural Modal Analysis and Synthesis for Free-Viewpoint Navigation in Dynamically Vibrating Scenes

Automne Petitjean1, 2, Yohan Poirier-Ginter3, 2, Ayush Tewari4, Guillaume Cordonnier2, George Drettakis2,
1ENS de Lyon, 2INRIA université côte d'azur, 3Université Laval, 4MIT CSAIL
Paper Code Group Publication Page

ModalNeRF allows physically-plausible synthesis of new motion in nerf reconstructed dynamic scenes.

Abstract

Recent advances in Neural Radiance Fields enable the capture of scenes with motion. However, editing the motion is hard; no existing method allows editing beyond the space of motion existing in the original video, nor editing based on physics.

We present the first approach that allows physically-based editing of motion in a scene captured with a single hand-held video camera, containing vibrating or periodic motion. We first introduce a Lagrangian representation, representing motion as the displacement of particles, which is learned while training a radiance field. We use these particles to create a continuous representation of motion over the sequence, which is then used to perform a modal analysis of the motion thanks to a Fourier transform on the particle displacement over time.

The resulting extracted modes allow motion synthesis, and easy editing of the motion, while inheriting the ability for free-viewpoint synthesis in the captured 3D scene from the radiance field. We demonstrate our new method on synthetic and real captured scenes.

Overview

Overview: We introduce a new Lagrangian, particle based representation of motion. Each particle has a position in canonical space and in deformed space. This allows us to deform rays during rendering and to easily perform modal analysis on the particles. From this modal analysis, a synthesis can be performed to generate a new deformation state than will be rendered easily. Once the model is trained, synthesizing and rendering new views takes up to 10 seconds.
